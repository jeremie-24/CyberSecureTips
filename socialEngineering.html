Social Engineering

Social engineering is the manipulation of people through the Internet to steal personal data. Malicious people trick others to divulge their confidential information. For instance, a social engineer could send a fake email to someone that asks to reset his password for a website. When the email’s receiver clicks on the link in the email, he is sent to a fake website that steals his password. Another common example is when a malicious person calls someone on a phone to “fix” a problem on their computer. The scammer pretends to be an employee from a known company like Microsoft and asks for personal information like a credit card number. Also, the scammer could make the computer’s owner follow a procedure to “fix” the problem. In reality, this procedure would give him remote access to the device.
