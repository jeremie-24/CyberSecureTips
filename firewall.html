Firewall

Posted by Jérémie Couturier on November 20, 2022

A firewall can filter the traffic of a device’s entry point. It protects the device by blocking any incoming and outgoing data that doesn’t respect the firewall’s rules.

It has predetermined rules, but a user can modify them to his liking. For instance, he could decide to block any data that isn’t coming from his private network or completely block any incoming data from any devices. With the latter, the device doesn’t have access to the Internet and the network, so it is like a device connected to no network.

Also, this security software can limit which program can access the Internet. Furthermore, it can limit the information your device sends and receives to keep your device as safe as possible. For those reasons and many more, a firewall is a useful tool to prevent hackers from intruding malicious data into your device or hijacking your computer.

Most computers have a built-in firewall, and most antivirus software have one available with it, so you should make use of one. Usually, firewalls are automatically enabled, and you don’t have to worry about it. Your device should already be safe, but you can tweak the settings to your liking.
