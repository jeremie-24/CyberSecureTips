Secure Passwords

Why Is a Secure Password Important?

Posted by Olivier Bourdel on October 6, 2022

Why?

A secure password is the only barrier between you and hackers, but what exactly are we protecting and why do we need to protect it?

The Key to the Precious

Passwords are almost always a must for any technological tool, and it turns out that certain tools are effortlessly exploitable. What if someone manages to get your bank information? They can wreak havoc with your money and by the time you notice your balance will probably be left in shambles. Or what if you work at a company with a database with sensitive information? Your password can mean the difference between a long prosperous career and a costly data leak.

The Key to your Things

If hackers can seize your password, they now hold an asset over you. Ransom is often heard of: hackers can lock you out of your social media account, your bank account or even your computer and only give you access back for a price. And what’s more, these ransoms will often make use of Bitcoin, an online currency that’s often impossible to trace transactions, so you could pay them, and they could disappear forever with no way of finding them.

The Key to You

If all else fails, most of your accounts can also hold precious data: your data. By collecting your personal information (your name, your address, your social security number and so on), your identity can be faked and used against your will. Bank accounts can be opened in your name for use in money laundering, a mortgage can be taken out in your name; the possibilities are terrifyingly endless.
